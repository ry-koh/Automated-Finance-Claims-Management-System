Step 1 of 2
Send a Telegram Message to Your Liaison

YOUR LIAISON
Finance Team Member

+ For each purchase: receipt photo + bank transaction screenshot
sent ✓✓
What each field means
Portfolio
Your portfolio group. Skip if your Liaison already knows.
CCA
Your CCA name. Skip if your Liaison already knows.
Claim Desc
Brief name of the event. E.g. "Disc Claim" or "Welfare RHingo Claim".
Others
Emails of others involved. Leave blank if none.
Receipts
For each purchase, send its receipt photo and its bank screenshot together. Don't send everything in a pile — your Liaison needs to match them up.
Missing a bank screenshot? Finance Team can't process the claim. Every purchase needs both.
Step 2 of 2
Send the Email When You Receive It

Finance Team Member

To: rh.finance@u.nus.edu

Subject: 2526-WELFARE-WELFARE COMM-001
COPY THIS SECTION

Dear Jun Kiat, attached is the claims for Disc Claim.

I, [YOUR FULL NAME], [MATRIC], hereby authorise Jun Kiat to collect reimbursement on my behalf.

PayNow: 9XXX XXXX  |  Total: $XX.XX

Your receipts are already attached to the Finance Team email you received
What you need to do
1
Create a NEW email
Do not reply — open a fresh compose window.
2
Copy To, CC, Subject
Use the exact values shown. Don't change the subject line.
3
Paste body & send
Copy the dotted section, paste as your email body, send.
Do not reply to the Finance Team email — create a brand new email to the Finance Director.
Subject line = your Reference Code. Keep it to follow up if needed.
After Step 2

✅

📸
Screenshot & compile
Finance Team screenshots your sent email, adds it to your documents, merges everything into one PDF.
🏛️
NUS OFN approval
Finance Office submits to NUS Office of Finance. Once approved, NUS reimburses the Finance Director.
💸
Finance Director pays you
After receiving the reimbursement from NUS, Finance Director pays you back via PayNow.
Expect at least a month. The process goes through NUS OFN which is outside Finance Team's control. Follow up with your Liaison using your Reference Code if it's been unusually long.
That's it!
You're done.
